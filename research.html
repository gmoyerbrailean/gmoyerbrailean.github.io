My research focuses on developing computational methods to asses the impact of genetic variants on organismal phenotypes. Our lab attempts to characterize the effect of variants on complex traits by examining changes in chromatin accessability and gene expression surrounding these variants. Through this functional annotation, variants associated with a certain trait or disease (e.g., through GWAS) can be identified as truly causal or not.

Projects

Annotation of regulatory variation

Functional genomics data is increasingly easier and less expensive to generate, especially when coupled with next-gen sequencing technologies. However, understanding the impact of a sequence change within this regions remains a difficult challenge to address. Using the CENTIPDE model and our newly developed QuASAR model, we have developed an approach to annotating functional regulatory regions and variation within them.

We have set up a UCSC browser hub with our annotations, available from the project page here: http://genome.grid.wayne.edu/centisnps/
